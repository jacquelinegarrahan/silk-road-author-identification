Post by: cabinfever20 on March 03, 2013, 03:32 am
Bitcoins are trading more like a commodity right now; as in supply vs demand.  As more people learn about the "value" of the BTC there is an explosion in the demand and a fixed supply meaning a jump in price.  Once something goes wrong (like maybe a person/corporation with a lot of money slowly buying up millions of $$ in BTC's then dumping them on the market to make quick money) then you may see the price drop by factors of 10 as loads of BTC's hit the market.  The good news is that many of the vendors on SR base their prices on local currency (now: 1g H = 8.8 BTC = $300 6mos ago: 1g H = 75 BTC = $300).

The best example that I can thing of is the apparent "explosion" in gold prices.  The problem is that Gold has been worth the same amount for hundreds of years.  For example If I went to a tailor in NYC and bought a top of the line custom suit in 1850 it may have cost me ~$20.00, but that was when when Gold was trading at ~$10/oz.  If I go to NYC today and buy a custom suit it may cost me $4000.00 but now Gold is trading at $2000/oz.  Even though the cost of the suit seems to have increased quite a bit it actually hasn't changed at all.
