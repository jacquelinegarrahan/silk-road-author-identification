1. Memorize the URL.  It's silkroadvb5piz3r.onion, and it should always begin with that.  Just always check the URL before you EVER enter ANY passwords or your PIN or order ANYTHING.  Other than that, you don't have much to worry about.

2. MtGox is legit, but I have no idea how it works for the UK.  I live in the US and I just used BitInstant to do a cash transfer.  You print off a slip after you fill out the form, take it to walmart, or walgreens, or CVS, or anywhere with moneygram.  You pick up the red phone, enter the numbers on the slip, talk to some Indians and repeat some more numbers on the slip, then pay the cashier at the store.  Half an hour later you have coins in your wallet.

3. Escrow basically means the vendor doesn't get your money until you do what's called "finalizing".  When you receive your order, you finalize, which grants the vendor their money.  Until you finalize, your money is in the middle, being held by SR until you finalize or file a complaint.  This keeps you safe from scams because if your product doesn't arrive you can file a dispute and usually get a refund instead of trying to convince a seller to give you your money back.

4. Ordering inside your own country is the safest way to go, especially with weed.  As long as the vendor vacuum seals and has good packaging, the smell is usually not a problem. The main issue comes with customs, which is only a problem when you ship internationally.  Other than that, the US Postal service usually doesn't find anything unless there's a reason for it to be suspicious (bad packaging, coming from the Netherlands, smell).  If possible, domestic is the way to go.  Always check out reviews though and see what people say about their packaging methods.

5. If by scanned you mean like an X-Ray, I'm pretty sure domestic orders don't pass through those, or else a lot more orders would be getting caught.  I could be very wrong though, like I said before, domestic is the safest by far.

6. If you're paranoid and want to be SUPER safe:
    1. Write "RETURN TO SENDER" in big letters on the package and DON'T OPEN IT.
    2. Wait a week
    3. If nobody comes knocking, you're safe, if they do, you have plausible deniability ("I have no idea what this address is, I'm not expecting anything so I planned to take it back to the post office tomorrow")  Usually, they're more interested in getting the package before it even gets to you than busting you with it.
