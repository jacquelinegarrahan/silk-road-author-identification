Actually only about 1 out of 3 people who try K will end up enjoying it according to a book on Ketamine I read.  It seems some people dig it but most people actually don't like it. 

That said, K can be extremely psychologically addicting and can cause some very problematic and lasting psychological issues that persist for weeks/months if it's used too frequently.  It's a dissociative and a lot of people start out using it to explore the weird psychedelic effects but end up dependent with it for escaping their psychological issues.   It gets pretty rough on the kidneys and the wallet as tolerance increases very quickly and soon you need 5-10x as much to get the same effects.  Of course with a new drug it's hard to not want to 'get to know it' but you need to have a limit and stick to it.  After you get to know it,  I find the best way to use K is just one or two doses in a session and then waiting a few months or even years before doing it again.  It has some therapeutic qualities in this way, hence why I think it demands a lot of respect.
