Title: Re: Recommend some documentaries/books?
Post by: abitpeckish on June 02, 2013, 01:11 am
Literature:

Free Will - Sam Harris
  - chances are you believe that you are the author of your own thoughts and actions. this is not even remotely as true as you believe it to be. Sam Harris lays out a devastatingly clear and concise (< 100 pages!) explanation as to why that is, what it means, and especially what it doesn't mean.

Zen and the Art of Motorcycle Maintenance - Robert Pirsig
  - this gets pretty dense on the philosophy, but the experience of reading it is sublime
  - this book changed me. it was slow, and it did not *fix* me, but it absolutely changed me

Catcher in the Rye - J.D. Salinger
  - i cry every time i read this, and it's always on the same page
  - this is a book everyone should read at least once every 10 years

100 Years of Solitude - Gabriel García Márquez
  - one of the most beautiful experiences I've ever read
  - there will be flying carpets within the first 100 pages, and you will believe they are real

Movies:

Where The Wild Things Are
  - this movie got a lot of shit, and none of it was deserved. it is brutally (but metaphorically) honest about growing up and learning to deal with the monsters of desire that most people are most of the time.
  - this is NOT a kids movie

Apocalypto
  - just ... fuck yeah

2001: A Space Odyssey
  - fuckin Kubrick man. You owe it to yourself to drop acid and watch this movie.
  - DO NOT spoil this movie for yourself by reading the plot.
  - it's too soon to tell, but I'm pretty sure this movie changed my life.
