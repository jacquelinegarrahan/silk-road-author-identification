All you would have to do to refute the supply claim would be to say that you could take 3 pills over 3 weeks, or 3 months, or even a year. It has to be proven that you intended to supply the drugs. More often this is done based on a valuation (which are always disgustingly inflated and again shouldn't be hard to defend against with a real expert valuation), but sometimes it is calculated by just weight or # of pills/vials. This again can be defended by proving that you did not intend to receive that # or weight. Or by proving that you had a more serious than normal habit.
Depends on the precedent for the particular substance. For possession and intent to supply there are guidelines but for importation (evasion of prohibition, NOT trafficking charge) there are not guidelines just case law.
You could also just claim that you were sure the pills/powders were legal. So long as there is no supply charge then this is not really a crime (point towards cases where people have been charged for possessing inactive material that they thought was drugs etc..).
