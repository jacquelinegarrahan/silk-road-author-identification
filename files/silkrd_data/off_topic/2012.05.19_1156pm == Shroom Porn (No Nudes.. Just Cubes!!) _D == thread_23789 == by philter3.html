Post by: philter3 on May 20, 2012, 01:32 am
Something really simple you can try.

Do your usual PF Tek style 1/2 pint jar grow. When they are fully colonized and you've got COMPLETE colonization on all the jars and pinning on 1/4-1/3 of them.. get yourself a clear plastic tub (about 16 liters or so) from the Dollar Store.

Spray paint the bottom and the lower 4 inches of the sides with flat black enamel spray paint. Do this to the OUTSIDE btw.

Take a metal cheese grater. Birth the cakes, and shred them like they are hunks of cheese with the grater into the tub. Level the surface nice and even.

Mist and cover. Remist gently every few days. You can also pasturize some vermiculite to lay over top as a simple casing layer if you like.

Wait. Watch. Enjoy. Yields are higher than a terrarium classic PF style and much more covert for closet use. This will allow you to gently work up to more intricate casing methods.

Quote from: moksha on May 20, 2012, 05:24 am
Thanks for the advice I've been reading into the terrarium tek the last few days as well, hoping to get a grow started in the next couple weeks.
A few quick questions if you didn't mind helping me out. Do you drill air holes in the tub you put them in? And are you putting down verm on the bottom then grating the cakes on top? I was just going to get a few cakes once they're completely colonized after about a week, and place them on moist verm but I'm liking your idea.

Oh and what did you mean by the term "pinning"? Just haven't come across it before, cheers for all the tips!

Air holes are not really necessary with small grows like this little tub setup. You are going to be opening it up every day or two to mist anyway. That's plenty of air exchange. All those factors about CO2 and pin-setting are way more complicated than you need to worry about with this little dealie. They ARE important in larger more sophisticated setups though.. I'm not dismissing them for that though.

You can put down verm on the bottom if you like. It will add some moisture retention qualities to your substrate. You can even layer verm, shreds, verm, shreds if you are feeling froggy. This is what I meant by the shredded cakes being a good gentle entryway into more sophisticated bulking/casing methods. You can ease into it.

There is no point in keeping the cakes whole.. distribute their total area by shredding instead of leaving them intact. Fruiting body mass and volume is a function of both substrate mass and substrate surface area. By shredding the mass stays the same, but the surface area increases dramatically.

Pinning is when you start to see the little bitty pre-shroom bodies forming.. like little worms, or dots on the surface of the cakes or your substrate. Pins become new fruiting bodies basically.
