Title: Re: why do people like benzos?
Post by: Ben on May 17, 2013, 01:23 am
People take benzo's for a very wide variety of situations.

The primary one is to cope, to function, to reduce irrational anxieties and such. This is bascially what the less-narcotic benzo's like xsanax were developed to do, and they often do this very well.

Another major application is as hypnotics, i.e. sleep aids, for which other benzo's prove quite effective in the short term, though tolerance usually developes quickly, making it only a temporary fix.

Lastly there is the strictly recreational use, where people take large doses of things like xanax to produce a feeling comparable to alcohol intoxication. This is not very common though since in most places alcohol is more readily and cheaply available.

Combination use is another thing entirely though: substances like xanax can be used to 'cancel' bad trips in many cases. People using psychedelics like lsd often keep some benzo's at hand in case their trip turns bad to have a way of stopping that and dozing off for a bit instead of riding out a negative experience.
