What's the best drug for you? For me, in order from least to most

Tobacco - at the bottom of the list is tobacco - never really did much for me and destroyed my health, wallet and was a fucking bitch to quit. (how people quit shit like heroin when I very easily could have killed myself with cigarettes is beyond me).

XTC - then there's XTC. I had a bad trip on XTC (yes, it happens) and I can't imagine getting anything useful from it, but it didn't do all the havoc tobacco did.

Marijuana - next is marijuana. marijuana has given me some insights, but for the most part I find them to be false. when i write supposedly genius things down, they never are the next morning. But some legitimate insight which is hard to put into words is possible.

PCP - PCP was, for the most part, just not pleasant. but as the only real hallucinogen I've done, and as a composer of electronic music, the primarily aural hallucinations of PCP have given me a lot of inspiration for writing music, even if it was just from the one trip where I did very little of the stuff.

Booze - Then there's alcohol. I have a lot of social anxiety so alcohol has allowed me to have some great times with friends and family and I'm grateful for it. Alcohol would probably be at the bottom of many people's list, but it's significantly improved the quality of my life.

Coffee - simply put, my best thought occur after I've had a couple cups of coffee. When I need to really work on a big project and I'm psyched about it, coffee makes me feel more creative and gets my thoughts going better than any drug I've tried so far. Not sure whether to put this above or below booze, though, as me having some really cool thoughts and ideas and the impulse to write hasn't really affected my quality of life yet.

In the works is mushrooms which I've ordered from here. And my ultimate drug goal is to do Ibogaine.

What about you guys? What drug has made your life better?

The first person to say DMT!

I am not sure (yet) if I would place DMT at the top of my list of 'healthy' drugs but it is certainly the substance which is the most mind-blowing. Yes, mushrooms/pscilocybin can have the effect of massively opening the mind to a whole range of metaphysical phenomena but DMT does something very different. For me, a strong hit of DMT is like being shot out of a cannon down a corridor in the library of forbidden knowledge at 1000mph, where one witnesses all kinds of stunning revelations that we don't have the capacity to interpret and are probably not meant to know about. Also whilst just sitting every other evening giving yourself micro-doses and maybe the odd mild dose can be a pleasant and not too overwhelming way to use DMT, I have found that any kind of regular usage of DMT has the effect of eroding the foundation stones of someones outlook and certainty about life. This is of course can be ultimately a positive process although not necessarily a comfortable one.

The only time have hit a totally euphoric state after a strong dose of DMT, is when I have vaped some after arriving home from being out clubbing on MDMA. Any time I have been straight and taken a strong dose, the experience is simply fucking mental. Amazing? Thrilling? Most certainly! But more in the same way as a bungee jump would be other than as a profound spiritual experience.......that is just my personal experience of course........but I would say that DMT is far too intense and far too short to be used as an ideal spiritual/mental development tool.

As for the 'best drugs' for a person:

The 'social' drugs such as alcohol, MDMA, cocaine, etc are all poisons which most of us find pleasurable to indulge in from time to time. Anyone who attaches any sense of real 'developmental' benefit to these things is on a very wrong path as far as I am concerned.

Weed/cannabis, can be used both as a social/pleasure substance and also as a psychoactive substance which can shift mental perception allowing for insights that the user would otherwise not have. However, this drug seems to focus the mind very much on the realm of the bodily senses (and the gratification thereof), which is its big limitation. Whilst providing it is used without tobacco products, THC is much healthier than any of the other 'social' drugs, these 'restricted' psycho-activity properties can have a crippling effect on an individuals mental/spiritual development, whilst fooling the individual into thinking that it is the opposite that is the case.

So obviously, the substances which can be most beneficial are the hallucinogenics which I can vouch have made my life 10 times more interesting and have assisted my spiritual/mental development massively, opening my mind to realities about the world that I would have otherwise remained completely ignorant to. I have used LSD, mushrooms, and in recent time, DMT. My favourite of all is probably the mushrooms as although they take the user on a mad rollercoaster ride similar to what LSD does, everything about mushrooms is so much more down to Earth whereas the paradigms/delusions generated by LSD tend to be less bound with natural energies, and much more persistent. Thus the LSD user has to spend more of his post trip time, living under his LSD trip generated illusions/delusions.

Now that I have discovered Silk Road, Mescaline could very much be on the menu but aside from all the other uncertainties......it does seem very pricey.
