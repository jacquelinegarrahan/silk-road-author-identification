alright seeing a lot of overlap on artists I already know. so here's my own list (with some prev. artists), with a bit of fun categorizing...these are some hymns that mused my mind

60s:
The Doors (Strange Days and The Doors are great)
Pink Floyd (obviously... DSoTM, Meddle, Atom Heart Mother, Animals... also listen to "Love Scene - Version 4")
Led Zeppelin
Jefferson Airplane (Surrealistic Pillows)
Jimi Hendrix (really almost anything you can get your hands on, you won't regret it)
The Mars Volta (well... not precise category, but still deserves to be here)
The Beatles (another given.. beware Revolution 9)
Comus (acid folk also happened guys...can get a bit "energetic")

The "Beats":
Bonobo (anything works)
Flying Lotus (beware Cosmogramma... July Heat and 1983 are nice ones)
Boards of Canada (some stuff beware... if you want a journey, listen to Disengage)
J Dilla (Donuts... sick production)
Onra (j dilla's vietnamese cousin)
Zero 7 (Simple Things)
Nujabes (Metaphorical Music)
DJ Krush (Jaku... only if ya dig Nujabes)
Amon Tobin (the drums can get intense)
The Avalanches (some of Since I Left You is nice)
Easy Star All-Stars (Dub Side of the Moon)
De Facto
Quantic

Getting jazzzzzier or funkier? (only sometimes when I trip):
Dave Brubeck (Time-out, of course)
Billie Holiday
Miles Davis
Kool & The Gang (Summer Madness is a great track)
John Coltrane (Interstellar Space... beware, a truly intense collection)
George Duke (Face the Music)
Jaco Pastorius ("Portrait of Tracy")
Nina Simone ("Ne Me Quitte Pas" is pleasant)

Classic:
Bach (Cello Suite no. 1 in G major)
Chopin ("Prelude In E Minor")
Erik Satie (his Gymnopedies)
Leo Brouwer (any of his solo guitar compositions... haven't heard much of his orchestra stuff)
James Tenney
Scriabin
