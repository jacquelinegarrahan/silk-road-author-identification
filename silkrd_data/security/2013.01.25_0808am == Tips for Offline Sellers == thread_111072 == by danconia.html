Title: Tips for Offline Sellers
Post by: danconia on January 25, 2013, 08:08 am
I know I'm not the only person on SR who re-sells to people in their local area.  I feel pretty safe about online activity (BTC, SR, and even delivery) but sometimes feel like the weak link in my chain is when I sell to people in my area.  For reference I live in the USA.

After a few months of selling I think there are a few tips that I can give up front:

1.) Try to have a stable job so that selling is only an "on the side" thing, so that you don't get desperate if something happens and you can't sell for a few months or more
2.) Try not to sell to people that you don't know well.  If you sell to people that you don't know well, then perhaps it's best to not sell out of your residence or wherever you keep your stash.
3.) Consider using an extra "pay-as-you-go" phone to do business and possibly a pseudonym for selling to people that you don't know that well.
4.) Obviously never volunteer any information to law enforcement.  If they want info out of you, then don't ever do it without a lawyer (and try not to talk to LE at all).  If they want to come into your place, tell them they need a warrant.

I have a question and would like to here some of your guys' tips:
1.) How hard is it typically for law enforcement to get a warrant to come into your residence?

PS For those of you who don't sell offline, please don't come into this thread bashing us for what we do.  Everyone has their different preferences and circumstances, and most offline sellers don't take the risks lightly.

Post by: The Advocate on February 02, 2013, 09:31 am
Quote from: danconia on January 25, 2013, 08:08 am
1.) How hard is it typically for law enforcement to get a warrant to come into your residence?

A search warrant for a a dwelling house that you currently reside within requires a judge to find "probable cause" of criminal activity.  Probable cause is a reasonable, articulatable,  suspicion that criminal activity is afoot.  It is sometimes described as a 10%-30% likelihood of the suspected crime being true.  LE learns which judges are lenient and which are more strict, so you can safely bet that they will seek out the easy sale by going to a more lenient judge.  The search warrant has to list the items that are expected to be found, as well as the places that will be searched.  The way LE finds probable cause is usually through the affidavit of a confidential informant.  The identity of the CI need not be known to the judge, or even law enforcement.  The evidence provided by the CI is generally in need of corroboration.  The more detailed the affidavit, the more effective it will be.  Whether a CI has been reliable in the past is also something judges look at when considering the issuance of a search warrant.  There are different types of evidence that can secure a search warrant, but in my experience as a criminal defense atty they usually involve some sort of informant, undercover agent, and surveillance.

It's important to have a criminal defense atty that is passionate about fighting for your rights and knowledgeable on how to make the evidence procured form the warrant inadmissible.
Hope this helps!

It's possible to sell IRL anonymously if you use bitcoins.
The difference is that you don't use the internet to advertise yourself (like here on SR), and don't have to use the mail.

Go to bars, partys and look for local dealers, find a way to contact them anonymously (ask for a phone number/e-mail while pretending to be a simple buyer, or find out where they live, or pay a bum to give them a package etc).  The dealer gets a package with a sample along with your e-mail address. If you must use a phone change it frequently, or use smstoemail services so the dealer can use his phone to send you an SMS, but you get it as an email without having to have a phone. If the dealer is interested describe bitcoin and how it works. You can use dead drops, after the dealer pays you drop the package and let the dealer know where he can find it.
If you already know some local dealer and you consider him serious enough,  you can give the first package for free. The dealer will be more willing to pay in advance for the next packages, and if he's indeed serious you can have an ongoing business for the price of that first package. At the end you can get out of the business with no loss if you simply don't deliver the last order before ending the business with him, but you can do the same after a couple of transactions. It shouldn't be a huge lost anyway you gain more with the business itself.

Dealing with users works almost the same, find whoever is likely to buy your product, approach them anonymously,  deliver by doing dead drops or mail. Leaving samples with your contact address around head shops, bars etc might also work but risky and it's quite a long shot.
It might be hard to find the first 5 buyers if you don't know anyone around, but after that they will either become a dealer and move your product or hand out your address for other users.
If you are really clueless look around on forums, music forums, club's websites etc, find eligible customers, approach them online etc.

Using Tor, avoiding security cameras, leaving your phone at home when doing the drops, and sticking to BTC and it's almost as safe as dealing on SR.
If BTC is a problem find someone who's willing to accept payments and convert it to BTC but you can loose some of your money this way.

Former LEO here.  Just wanted to add a couple of things.  So many good points have already been made.

1.  Don't sell out of your house.  In most states, that is a whole separate felony, and opens your whole home to search and seizure.  If you own a gun, the gun becomes part of the drug felony.  If you pay rent, the rent was paid to maintain a drug business.  If you sell out of your home, eventually, the police will arrest one of your buyers, who will give you up to stay out of jail.  They will give him money, he will go buy from you, and the SWAT team will follow him.  Cops aren't smart, and narcs are even less so.  The buy/bust routine is the only string on their banjo.  But, keeping your home (any home, any building you maintain) out of the equation will insulate you from a host of felonies.

2.  Driving with drugs has already been covered, but never, and this sounds obvious, but never consent to a search.  In the quiet corner of your home you might say, "I would *never* allow LE to search my car", but on the side of the road, I dunno what it is, but people do.  If LE has to ask, it means they don't have legal cause.  So, always say no, and stick to your guns.  Be aware that they may come back with, "okay, it'll just be a minute," while they're calling for a drug dog.  At that point, let the money roll.  Making you wait may not be legal, if the point is just to get the dog, and the dog might not be available.  Depending on what you're carrying, a dog might be useless.  Just do not freak out.  Whatever happens, you're going to get through this, and if things go badly, your lawyer will challenge the wait, he'll challenge the dog, and he'll challenge the stop. 

3.  Guns are a tricky issue.  The Supreme Court ruled that where there are drugs, LE can assume there are also guns.  That is problematic for a number of obvious reasons.  A number of different opinions have been offered here, and you will have to decide for yourself what course to take.  I would simply add this:  Gunfighting is no simple thing.  If you decide to carry a gun, I would encourage you to put the time and effort into knowing how to use it.  The average gunfight last 2.5 seconds and expends 1.5 rounds, at an average distance of less than 3 feet.  That's not a lot of time or space to put your life into, so you'd better know what you're doing.  It's almost never a good idea to shoot at the cops.  Don't ever shoot yourself, because it's never, ever that bad.  Enough about guns.

4.  Keep your stashes small.  Or, at the very least, keep your large stashes of drugs and money hidden very well.  The worst thing that can happen to you, from a legal standpoint, is for local authorities to give up your case to the feds.  They will only do that if the feds care, and they will only do that if you are either well-connected or a big player.  They will decide the former based on their intelligence or your rolodex, and they will decide the latter based on your stash.  Don't be the guy with half a million dollars stuffed in his mattress.  It will not go well for you.  Don't be the guy with 20 kilos of white in what serves for the backseat of the Lamborghini parked in the garage.  Those cases will go federal, and those defendants will go to prison.  Hide your money, and drive a Nissan.

Hope that adds and doesn't detract.
