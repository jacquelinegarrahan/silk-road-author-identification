Question: If you don't use PGP who can see your address and where/when?

When you send your address to a vendor TSR encrypts it until the vendor views it and permanently deletes it once the vendor marks the shipment as in transit.

However, if either the silk road as a whole or a specific vendor's account were compromised by law enforcement (unlikely, but everything is possible) they theoretically could view buyer addresses before the system deletes them.

By encrypting your address with the vendor's public key you ensure that the only person that is able to view your address/sensitive info is the vendor you intend to see it---even if the silk road as a whole were compromised for whatever reason.

Basically it's just another layer of security, and there's no reason to take any unnecessary chances in this business.
