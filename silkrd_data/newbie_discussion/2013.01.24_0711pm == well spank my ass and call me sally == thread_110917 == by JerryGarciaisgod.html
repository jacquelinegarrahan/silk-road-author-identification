Why the paranoia?r
Because being crazy is better than being in jail.
My rationale is this: Aside from the actual shipment, buying the bitcoins is potentially the least secure part of the transaction. For those of us who don't deal, these measures are for personal peace of mind more than anything, as well as the fact that no amount of security is too much, so long as it does impede leading a healthy productive life. For those who do delve into dealing drugs, however, these measures are not really even that paranoid, since the law has this obnoxious tendency of expending enormous amounts of time and energy hunting down drug dealers and locking them up in cages. If the police (or God forbid the DEA) notice a significant increase of say, high quality MDMA in the area, they might endeavor to shut that operation down. Because the law affords us certain rights and liberties, this can sometimes be a difficult task. Basically, they might find out you're dealing, or have a strong hunch pointing in your direction, but often enough won't have enough evidence to act. Thus, making sure things like BTC purchases are well disguised is a extremely worth-while thing to do. Even if no single piece of evidence is sufficient for a warrant, an preponderance of circumstantials can be enough to convince a judge which is all that really matters where warrants are concerned.
This seems like a long response now that I look at it. Turns out Tramadol makes me speedy as shit.

Source: My OG of a law professor + my own experience and intuition.
