Hey hopeful,

Black tar heroin produced in Mexico and is less refined than powdered heroin. BTH is available on the West Coast because the Mexicans control the drug trade to the Western United States. In places like Denver, most of the heroin available is tar, but I have heard there is some powdered heroin #4 available also.

The black tar variety is black (or at least quite dark) and less refined because the producers are using a variation of an older process that was developed back in the late 1800's. I am fairly certain the process that the Mexican cartels are using to acetylate their morphine is a variation on the first process ever discovered to produce heroin. They use acetic acid (basically really concentrated vinegar) rather than acetic anhydride to acetylate morphine.

An interesting side effect of the more primitive process used to produce BTH is that it leaves a large amount of monoacetylmorphine derivatives in the end product (6-MAM, 3-MAM) which I have heard causes a more intense "rush" when injected. The 6-MAM in particular is quite potent and very quick to cross the blood-brain barrier. 

The powdered heroin that is available on the east coast is usually from Columbia or Asia (Southeast Asia or Southwest Asia). From what I have heard and read, the powdered #4 heroin is cleaner than tar, but in terms of potency, tar and powder are fairly close these days.

I was always a little confused by the numbering system for heroin, but here is my understanding of it:
#1 = Opium (not heroin, so that doesn't make much sense)
#2 = Morphine  (again, still not heroin, so I dunno, could be wrong)
#3 = Heroin freebase (I hear this is what they get in the UK/Europe. It's freebase heroin and requires acidification to make it injectable. That's why ppl in the UK/Europe mix in some citric acid with their heroin before injection. The acid combines with the freebase heroin to make heroin hydrochloride. It's also supposed to be good for smoking in its freebase form -- just as freebase cocaine (crack) is)
#4 = Heroin HCl (heroin hydrochloride, the most refined heroin)

Before the Mexicans got as big into the heroin trade and flooded the western US with black tar, virtually all of the heroin in the USA came from the Golden Triangle region of Southeast Asia. The hill tribes there grow poppies and harvest opium that they sell to traders who in turn pass it along to wholesale distributors who operate laboratories to process the crude opium into morphine and then to heroin. I've seen reports that state a lot of the SEA heroin gets imported by Asian gangs through Western Canada, but I'm no expert.

What I find really interesting is that back from the 1970's to the mid 1990's, virtually all of the heroin on the east coast was coming from the Golden Triangle and was controlled by a Burmese warlord named Khun Sa. He was apparently using funds from drug trafficking to finance a guerrilla army fighting for an independent Shan state. I saw a documentary about the guy recently and it was very interesting. For several decades, this guy was called the "Opium King" and was basically behind like 90% of the heroin available on the east coast of the United States and much of the heroin available across the globe.

One thing I saw about this guy that really sums up the farce that is the "war on drugs" is how the US and Australian governments handled his offer to cease selling his product on the open market. Here's a copy/paste from Wikipedia that explains what he offered:

"In 1988, Khun Sa was interviewed by Australian journalist Stephen Rice, who had crossed the border from Thailand into Burma illegally. Khun Sa offered to sell his entire heroin crop to the Australian Government for A$ 50m a year for the next eight years, a move that would have virtually stopped the heroin trade into both Australia and the United States overnight. The Australian Government rejected the offer, with Senator Gareth Evans declaring: The Australian Government is simply not in the business of paying criminals to refrain from criminal activity.

In 1989, Khun Sa was charged by a New York court for trying to import 1,000 tons of heroin. By then he had proposed the USA buy his entire opium production or he would sell it on the international narcotics market."

That's right, the governments of the United States and Australia had the opportunity to cut off virtually ALL of the heroin flowing into their respective countries overnight for a small fraction of what they pay fighting their "war on drugs"! Of course they didn't accept the offer. I suppose it should be left as an exercise for the reader to deduce why they chose not to.

One thing I'd like to know is what the prevalence of Afghan heroin in the United States is these days. Near the end of the Taliban's reign, the production of opium in Afghanistan was at an all-time low. After NATO commenced the "war on terror" in Afghanistan, the production of heroin has increased like 40-50 fold. The most common figure that you hear getting thrown around is that Afghanistan produces 90% of the world's opium supply. My understanding is that the majority of that ends up in Europe, but I'm sure some of it has to be available in the United States.
