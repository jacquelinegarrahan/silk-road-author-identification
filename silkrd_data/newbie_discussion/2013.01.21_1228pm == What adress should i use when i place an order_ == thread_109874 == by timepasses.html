Title: Re: What adress should i use when i place an order?
Post by: Tarnished on January 21, 2013, 11:53 pm
Sending to a vacant house is an instant and obvious red flag. The postman knows what houses are unoccupied.

If you rent where you live, and the vendor has good stealth, you can usually just have it sent directly to you. If you make up a name, that can result in the package getting flagged.

The key is to not have it sent with a shipping method requiring a signature. Then if the postman asks for a signature, you know the stealth has been penetrated, and you just refuse to sign (and never have any contraband sent to your address ever again).

If you own where you live, then you can have asset forfeiture risks, especially if you're ordering greater than personal use quantities. In that case, get a P.O. Box.

If you get the box under a fake name, then you're adding mail fraud to your crimes, so that's probably a mistake.

Finally, if you can't handle the possible worst case scenario, don't buy contraband in the mail.

It is best to use a VALID address and a VALID name.  If the name does not match the name on the mailbox, it might be rejected and returned (to a nonexistant address in most cases).  The vendor has a right to refuse a reship if you are not using a valid address or name. 
If you order Express, ask the vendor to please mark it for "Signature not required".  Some postmen ignore this because they are on autopilot, so if you are asked to sign, use your best judgement.  You can refuse or say return to sender, but you are assuming a loss when you do that. 
If you have a PO Box, don't be surprised if you receive a note to pick up your package at the desk.  Most mail boxes are not large enough for the larger Express and Priority envelopes. 
If you receive a package that seems suspicious, (asking for signature on non signature package, being asked to wait at the PO while someone searches for your package, or if you think you are being watched), many other buyers suggest simply marking out the address and writing "return to sender". 
These are just some things to consider.

It is best to use PGP encryption for your name and address when ordering.  LE cannot break the encryption without your key. 
Note: when you place your order, your information (address, name, any notes on the order) are only visible to the vendor until they place the order in transit.  Once the order is placed in transit, that information disappears and is never accessible.
You can also use privnote for this information, which is only accessible one time and then burned.  But this is not encrypted, PGP is the way to go.
