Man, this is bringing back memories of my first purchase (not that it was all that long ago...I just seem to have spent much of the intervening time intoxicated, shockingly, so my sense of time may be just a little skewed...).  It took me days to work out the whole system, learn about Tor, get BTC, have them transferred to my account, contact a vendor and place an order, so forth--each step becoming more convinced that something was going to go horribly wrong and I was going to be burned out of my cash (or worse) as punishment for letting myself get suckered into world that seemed to violate so many long-held notions of the way things "have" to be.  Even after ordering, I mentally prepared myself for a LOOOONG wait (if my order even showed up at all, which I was doubtful it would).  I readied myself for many days of disappointment from the repeated opening of an empty mailbox, and imagined with dreadful anticipation writing a message to the vendor with whom I'd placed my order, in which I'd bashfully mention that my order never showed up and ask with supreme helplessness what he suggest I do ("fuck off" being my anticipated answer, since I had FE'd in order to get my order accepted in the first place).

And yet, only two days after pressing that order button for the first time, I opened my mailbox and gasped when a small padded envelope sat there.  Couldn't believe my eyes--not when I opened the mailbox, not when I tore apart the package and five beautiful tablets were revealed inside (underneath layers of stealth, of course), not when I Googled the imprint to confirm their authenticity...but I couldn't HELP but believe them when my vision finally started to blur with the warmth of intoxication, and I knew that I had found a place that was very unique and very, very special.  That moment will, of course, always be amongst the best of my highs, because it was about so much more than taking the drug itself (a drug which I had previously desired, but never had access to); it was about the validation of the effort--minor though it may have been--to discover the Silk Road and join the ranks of its buyers, and the thrill of discovery of a world that may very well defy the way things HAVE to be, yes, but that has instead captured with remarkable consistency (despite the flaws and imperfections that we all know exist) the way things SHOULD be--and CAN be, thanks to the efforts of enough like-minded individuals. 

I know I'm getting a little lyrical about an entity that many would still condemn, but, in all my experience, I've never seen a place (physical or ethereal) where the facilitation of drug use was accompanied so thoroughly by attention to safety, equanimity, and community.  And so, welcome to the Road--hope it treats you as well as it's treated me, and that you do your best to sustain it as the kind of place we all hope it can be, with all its promise fulfilled.
