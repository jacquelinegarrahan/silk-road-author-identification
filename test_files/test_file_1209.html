Basically a domestic package sent via UPS with 2 pounds of weed is intercepted and the cops setup a controlled delivery. An undercover agent pretending to be someone from the neighborhood walks up to the house holding the package, claiming that it was accidentally delivered to him. He asks the guy standing on the front porch if he wants the package and he says 'Yea I need that package' and offers to pay $40 to the guy as a thank you. The undercover doing the delivery passes him the package (he isn't asked to sign or anything), and then immediately grabs his arm and slams him to the ground.

Cops run in from everywhere and search the guys house and then question him. He denies knowing anything about the package, and the name on the package doesn't belong to anyone at the house. They open the package to reveal 2 pounds of weed sewn inside a pillow.

They then start interrogating him aggressively saying he's going down for the weed while he keeps denying any knowledge of it, until they threaten to arrest his girlfriend instead (the owner of the house) and he finally admits it's his and they arrest him and take him away.

Before the scene ends they show the wristband he was wearing that says 'winners don't do drugs' and have a laugh about it.

Lessons learned from this:
1. Don't trust anyone who isn't the mailman delivering a package to your house. Let the order go it isn't worth your freedom.
2. Don't say anything dumb like 'Yea I need that package', if you absolutely take a package say something like 'Look I wasn't expecting anything it could be a mistake but I guess I'll take it if it's addressed to my house'.
3. Don't stand around on your porch waiting for a delivery.
4. Don't crack under pressure and admit any knowledge of the package to police, even if they threaten to arrest your girlfriend. Cops do this kind of shit all the time, just make sure your girl has no idea what you do so she can't be a liability to your freedom.
